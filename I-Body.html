    Foundation of a Zoo: A Refuge for Conservation and Education

    The foundation of a zoo represents a significant milestone in promoting wildlife conservation and environmental education. This project, which typically involves collaboration among biologists, veterinarians, and conservationists, aims primarily to provide a safe haven for endangered species and those in need of rehabilitation. Additionally, modern zoos strive to be educational centers that inspire visitors to value and protect the planet's biodiversity. From initial planning to public opening, the creation of a zoo requires careful consideration of aspects such as designing habitats that mimic the animals' natural environment, implementing captive breeding programs, and organizing educational activities for all ages.

    Bird Conservation

    Bird conservation is essential for maintaining biodiversity and ecosystem health. Birds play vital roles, such as plant pollination, seed dispersal, and pest control, making them key indicators of environmental health. However, many bird species face serious threats, including habitat loss, climate change, illegal hunting, and pollution. To address these challenges, we have decided to breed the most threatened species.
